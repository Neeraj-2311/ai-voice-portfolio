## The context

Built at Mindcraft as one of three voice automation flows I shipped there, alongside a wider portfolio of Python scripts, Node microservices, and low-code workflows aimed at cutting operational time. The voice agent itself ran on a whitelabel platform (ElevenLabs / Retell). The engineering story is the orchestration around it.

## The problem

A small team needed to call hundreds of contacts to qualify leads, gather a few structured fields per call, and update their CRM. They didn't have headcount for it. Off-the-shelf dialers lacked decent voice agents, and the voice agent platforms didn't integrate cleanly with where the data actually lived: Google Sheets.

## The approach

Built a thin orchestration layer that treats Google Sheets as the source of truth for the contact list and the destination for outcomes. A Python worker reads the sheet, queues calls through a telephony provider, hands each call to the whitelabel voice agent with the contact's row context injected as a system prompt, then captures the structured outcome and writes it back to the sheet in real time.

The agent opens the call with the contact's name and reason for calling, runs through a short script, listens for objections, and ends with a structured handoff that the worker parses into sheet columns. Failures (no answer, voicemail, hung up, gatekeeper) are logged with reason codes so the team can re-target manually.

## Tech decisions worth noting

- **Used Google Sheets as the database.** Terrible at scale, perfect for a 200-row campaign and zero onboarding for the team.
- **Constrained the voice agent to strict structured output.** Every call ends by emitting JSON the worker parses. No LLM-judges-the-call ambiguity.
- **Ran a cron-driven worker rather than a UI.** Fewer moving parts, easy to debug, runs reliably overnight.

## Outcome

Handled ~100 calls in a single bulk campaign, cutting a multi-day manual dialing process into an overnight batch. Operators stopped touching the dialer and started reviewing a sheet of structured outcomes the next morning. Reused the same orchestration pattern across two further Mindcraft automation flows.

## What I learned

For cold calls, voice agent quality matters less than people think. What matters is the structured output contract and the recovery path when things go off-script. Most engineering time went into handling failure modes, silence, wrong number, gatekeeper, rather than improving the conversation itself.
